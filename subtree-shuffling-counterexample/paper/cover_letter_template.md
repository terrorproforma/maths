Dear Editors,

Please consider the manuscript **“An Explicit Fixed-Parameter Obstruction to Subtree Shuffling”**.

The paper gives a fixed-parameter counterexample to the subtree-shuffling conjecture of Bisi, Dyszewski, Gantert, Johnston, Prochno, and Schmid. The explicit parameters are `d=7` and `p=15`, and the failure occurs for every number of internal vertices `k=3m`. The proof constructs an exact integer vector in the left kernel of the tree-by-shuffle-class incidence matrix whose coordinate sum is nonzero.

The main technical ingredient is a weighted Horner suspension that converts a three-dimensional noninjective Keller map into a degree-seven homogeneous map in fifteen variables with nilpotent nonlinear Jacobian. Lagrange inversion supplies infinitely many nonzero inverse coefficients, and the published shuffle lemma and tree-inverse formula convert them into the incidence-matrix separators.

The repository accompanying the submission contains the complete LaTeX source, exact SymPy checks, regression tests, derivations, and a reproducible CI build.
